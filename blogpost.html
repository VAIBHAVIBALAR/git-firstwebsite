Pursed-lip breathing

 Pursed-lip breathing is a simple technique which consists of inhaling through the nose and exhaling through the mouth with pursued lips. A patient is instructed to inhale through the nose for several seconds with the mouth closed and then exhale slowly over 4 to 6 seconds through lips held in a whistling or kissing position. This can be done with or without abdominal muscle contraction-Ellen Hillegass. Essentials of Cardiopulmonary Physical Therapy. 4th edition. Elsevier. 2017
